To explain the core of who I am in a sentence: I solve problems. 
It's what makes me, me. 

Allow me to elaborate.... when I was in High School, my 3 of my good friends were in a Punk band. We all thought they were going to make it big, but they needed exposure. I said "You guys need a website!." I had no idea how to do that. I taught myself basic HTML(4), CSS, and Flash (yeah, I know). I went out and bought books, since there wasn't any solid learning material online in 2000. Within a month, I had their site up with animations and previews of their music.
Fast-forward to 2012... I started a job continuing my path of desktop support at a small but rapidly growing organization. Within the first month I decided to learn batch scripting to automate multi-step software installs and configurations, freeing us from hours of work each week. I was quickly promoted to a Senior / Lead role within the year. To continue to improve our workflow efficiency, I learned PowerShell and was able to leverage that gained knowledge to free up thousands of man-hours via scripting by writing GUI tools via combining WPF/.NET Framework + Powershell, and setting up company-wide software deployments . Without this, our team would have needed to add many more people than we could afford.
Soon I realized that development had become my primary role, soon I decided that I should take the plunge and go back to school, and after training my team members to their satisfaction, I pulled the plug and restarted my education. After completing my education at Coding Dojo, I feel like I've created a great stepping stone towards my future.
